Early Life

  • 1940 - Born in San Francisco, California
  • 1959 - Lee moved to Hong Kong after birth. During his teenage years, he constantly got into street fights, and had gotten to a point where organized crime members were involved. In fear of his safety, his parents sent him back to the US. He was 18.
  • 1961 - Following a short stay in San Francisco, Lee relocated to Seattle, where he attended high school, and enrolled in the University of Washington. He majored in drama.
  • 1964 - Lee married his college sweetheart Linda Emery. They had two children, Brandon and Shannon.

Martial Arts Career

  • 1959 - Lee began teaching martial arts, which he called "Jun Fan Kung Fu", in Seattle.
  • 1964 - He dropped out of college and moved to Oakland to work with then renowed martial artist James Yimm Lee. Together, they founded the second Jun Fan martial art studio in Oakland. The same year, invited by famous American martial artist Ed Parker, Lee appeared in Long Beach International Karate Championships. He was allegedly "discovered" by Hollywood.
  • 1967 - Lee invented Jeet Kune Do. Lee emphasised what he called "the style of no style". This consisted of getting rid of the formalised approach which Lee claimed was indicative of traditional styles.
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnant wife, and flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program. Over the next 16 years, his team breeds 6,000 different strains of disease resistent wheat - including different varieties for each major climate on Earth.
